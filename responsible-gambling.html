Responsible Gambling

⚠️ Warning: Gambling can be addictive. Only play with money you can afford to lose. You must be 18 or older to bet or play casino games.

8 Tips for Safe Gambling

  • Set a fixed monthly budget and never exceed it, even after a loss.
  • Treat gambling as entertainment — not a way to earn income.
  • Set time limits for every session and respect them.
  • Never chase losses; walk away when your budget is gone.
  • Do not gamble under the influence of alcohol or drugs.
  • Keep gambling separate from rent, bills and savings.
  • Take regular breaks — step away for at least 30 minutes every session.
  • Talk openly with family and friends if your play worries you.

8 Signs of Gambling Addiction

  • Playing for longer than you planned on most sessions.
  • Thinking about gambling even when doing other activities.
  • Borrowing money or using credit to fund your play.
  • Lying to family or friends about the time and money you spend.
  • Trying repeatedly to stop or cut down without success.
  • Feeling restless or irritable when not gambling.
  • Gambling as a way to escape stress or negative feelings.
  • Financial trouble, missed work or damaged relationships due to play.
